CAUSES OF AIR POLLUTION IN INDIA

Vehicle emissions :

India has one of the largest and fastest-growing fleets of vehicles in the world, and many of these vehicles are poorly maintained and emit high levels of pollutants.

Industrial emissions :

India is a major industrial power, and many of its factories emit large amounts of pollutants into the air.

Biomass burning :

Biomass burning is the burning of organic materials such as wood, crop residues, and dung for cooking, heating, and other purposes. This is a common practice in rural India, and it releases a significant amount of pollutants into the air. India is undergoing rapid urbanization, and construction activities are a major source of dust pollution.

Geography :

India has a unique geography that can trap pollutants in the atmosphere. For example, the Himalayas to the north prevent pollutants from dispersing, and the Bay of Bengal to the east can create a humid environment that favors the formation of smog.

The effects of air pollution in India are severe. Air pollution is estimated to cause over 1 million deaths in India each year, and it is a major risk factor for respiratory diseases, heart disease, and cancer. Air pollution also has a significant impact on the environment, damaging crops and forests and reducing visibility.

CONSEQUENCES OF AIR POLLUTION

Health impacts :

  • Respiratory problems: Air pollution can irritate the lungs and airways, leading to symptoms such as coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath. Air pollution can also aggravate existing respiratory conditions, such as asthma and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD).
  • Heart disease: Air pollution can damage the heart and blood vessels, increasing the risk of heart attack, stroke, and other cardiovascular problems.
  • Cancer: Air pollution is a known carcinogen, and it has been linked to an increased risk of lung cancer, bladder cancer, and other types of cancer.

    • Environmental impacts :

  • Damage to crops and forests: Air pollution can damage crops and forests, reducing yields and biodiversity.
  • Reduced visibility: Air pollution can reduce visibility, making it difficult to drive and fly.
  • Acid rain: Air pollution can lead to acid rain, which can damage buildings, lakes, and forests.

    • Economic impacts :

  • Air pollution also has a significant economic impact. Air pollution-related health problems can lead to lost productivity and increased healthcare costs. Air pollution can also damage crops and forests, leading to economic losses for farmers and businesses.

    • Social impacts :

  • Air pollution can also have a significant social impact. Air pollution-related health problems can lead to social isolation and decreased quality of life. Air pollution can also worsen social inequality, as poorer communities are often more exposed to air pollution.
